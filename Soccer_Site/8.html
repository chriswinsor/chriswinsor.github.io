Coaching Best Practices

Click on each of the Coaching Best Practices for this age group. When you have revealed all of them you will be able to move on.

A Community of Learners - Talk to the club's leadership and other experienced coaches at your club about what works for them. You will have access to years of hands on coaching experience right at your fingertips. Also, you can be a resource to other coaches. Put the 'U' & 'I' in community!
Begin with an End in Mind - Session Goal? - Spend time organizing what you would like your team to do for each practice. Have a plan in mind to maximize your activity. Use and explore 'The Library' on page 27.
Super Role-Model - It's important that you build positive relationships with your players. Work hard to develop a positive rapport while establishing your expectations for your environment.
Groundhog Day? That's ok! - For the younger age categories, don't be afraid to repeat the same exercises from week to week as long as the kids enjoy it. Repetition, or the familiar, can be comforting/reassuring for some kids. Repetition is also one of the 13 pillars of how we learn.
Timing is Everything - Maximum Participation! - Use structured activity for at least 50 minutes of an hour long practice. After a few water breaks and a scrimmage, you should hopefully have some tired and happy soccer players.
Who's Turn is it Anyway? - Limit long lines for activities. Kids standing around waiting for their turn aren't learning anything and will only get bored, distracted, and restless which may cause management issues for you.
