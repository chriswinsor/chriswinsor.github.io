Adapting a Session

Being able to adapt a session allows activity to be less tedious while boosting player engagement and development.

Click on the letters below to learn how to get D-MOST out of your session. Then click 'Next' to try an on-field example.

Distance - or size of playing space, width of gates, length of spacing, etc

Movement - increase the complexity of the task by increasing Agility, Balance, Coordination & Speed (ABCS)

Opposition - introduce some passive pressure or live defender(s)

Surface - increase the complexity of the activity by using a different surface (inside, sole, outside, heel, toe, laces, thigh, chest thigh)

Time - minimize time towards a challenge ie, How many passes can you make in 30 seconds?
